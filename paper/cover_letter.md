# Cover Letter

Dear Editors of *Applied Soft Computing*,

Please consider my manuscript entitled "Support-Aware Neuro-Memory Reinforcement Learning under Support Shift: A Soft-Computing Boundary Analysis" for publication in *Applied Soft Computing*.

This manuscript studies a hybrid soft-computing reinforcement-learning setting in which a neural value approximator is combined with exact-state memory, fuzzy support- and uncertainty-aware arbitration, and explicit support abstention. The paper does not claim a universally superior algorithm. It provides a reproducible boundary analysis of when memorized evidence and adaptive arbitration help, when zero support forces unreliable extrapolation, and when a declared fallback is preferable.

The study is relevant to *Applied Soft Computing* because it addresses a core issue in hybrid intelligent decision systems: how to combine neural approximation, explicit memory, and uncertainty-aware support information when the evaluation distribution differs from the observed training support. This framing connects directly to soft-computing themes such as robust learning under uncertainty, autonomous reasoning, intelligent agents, and decision-support systems.

This submission includes a controlled application-inspired navigation case with recurring training goals, deployment goal shift, risk zones, collisions, and a safe hold action. Thirty matched seeds compare tabular Q-learning, DQN, fixed mixing, count gating, support abstention, and the fuzzy adaptive gate. The study also reports unsupported-state ratio, branch use, abstention, exact-memory size, approximate memory footprint, and action-decision time. The fuzzy method improves selected DQN comparisons but does not generally outperform count gating, tabular learning, or support abstention; this mixed evidence is reported directly.

The manuscript also emphasizes reproducibility. Public materials include source code, JSON configurations, raw seed-level results, generated tables, vector figures, tests, package versions, provenance files, and audit scripts. The repository is available at https://github.com/ErcanErkalkan/confidence-gated-q, and the persistent artifact concept DOI is https://doi.org/10.5281/zenodo.20578927.

The claims distinguish development selection, independent validation, confirmatory task expansion, post hoc diagnosis, confirmation after diagnostic discovery, application validation, and descriptive cost analysis. Negative results, heterogeneous outcomes, and computational limitations are stated explicitly.

This manuscript is original, has not been published previously, and is not under consideration elsewhere. The author declares no conflict of interest.
