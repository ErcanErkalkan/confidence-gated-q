# Verified Results Summary

All values below come from committed result CSVs generated by the experiment
pipeline. The machine-readable source is
`paper/generated/verified_claims.csv`.

## DQN Development and Validation

- Development tuning: 245 runs, 92,690 raw rows, audit `PASS`.
- Selected candidate: `vanilla_dqn_buffer100k`.
- Independent validation: 440 runs, 190,902 raw rows, audit `PASS`.
- The validated DQN has a better descriptive mean environment rank than the
  original DQN (`1.25` versus `1.75`).
- No per-environment validated-versus-original contrast survives Holm
  correction.

## Confirmatory Compact Expansion

- Seven environments, seven methods, 30 seeds.
- 1,470 runs, 663,483 raw rows, audit `PASS`.

Count gate versus validated DQN, return AUC:

- FrozenLake 4x4: `+0.316`, bootstrap 95% CI `[0.268, 0.365]`,
  t-Holm `p=7.78e-12`.
- FrozenLake 8x8: `+0.027`, `[0.014, 0.041]`, t-Holm `p=0.0126`.
- CliffWalking: `+143.328`, `[113.611, 173.089]`,
  t-Holm `p=6.53e-9`.
- Held-out FourRooms sizes 7, 9, and 11: no corrected improvement.
- Taxi: mean `-11.886`, CI `[-23.866, -0.265]`, but t-Holm `p=0.861`.

Count gate versus tabular Q-learning:

- Taxi: `+154.379`, `[135.755, 173.004]`, t-Holm `p=1.62e-14`.
- FourRooms 7: `-1.149`; FourRooms 9: `-1.231`; FourRooms 11:
  `-1.584`. All 30 pairs favor tabular Q-learning in every FourRooms task.
- No general tabular superiority claim for count gating is supported.

## Support-Abstention Replication

- Three FourRooms tasks use seeds `300-329`.
- MiniGrid Empty-5x5 uses seeds `400-429`.
- 840 runs, 359,339 raw rows, audit `PASS`.

Support abstention versus count gating:

- FourRooms 7: `+1.164`, `[1.117, 1.211]`.
- FourRooms 9: `+1.190`, `[1.142, 1.238]`.
- FourRooms 11: `+1.625`, `[1.560, 1.689]`.
- All 30 pairs favor abstention for all three FourRooms sizes.
- Mean-based Holm values are below `8.0e-28`; Wilcoxon-Holm is `3.73e-8`.
- MiniGrid Empty-5x5: `+0.019`, `[-0.034, 0.070]`, t-Holm `p=1`.

The result confirms a specific support-shift repair. It does not establish
universal superiority or improvement over tabular Q-learning.

## Extended MiniGrid Diagnostic

- Six environments, seven methods, ten seeds.
- 420 runs, 215,786 raw rows, audit `PASS`.
- Count gating improves over validated DQN after Holm correction only on
  Empty 8x8: `+0.596`, `[0.442, 0.750]`, `p=0.000761`.
- Tabular Q-learning has the best descriptive mean environment rank (`1.58`).
- DoorKey and MiniGrid FourRooms results expose additional failures.
- The family remains a post hoc diagnostic and is not pooled with compact
  confirmation.

## Application Navigation Case Study

- One controlled deployment-goal-shift environment, six methods, 30 seeds.
- 180 runs, 81,922 raw rows, audit `PASS`.
- Evaluation decisions are unsupported on about `0.392` of steps.
- Fuzzy versus validated DQN AUC: `+0.575`, bootstrap 95% CI
  `[0.152, 0.987]`, t-Holm `p=0.0256`.
- Fuzzy versus count AUC: `+0.211`, `[-0.171, 0.626]`; not significant.
- Fuzzy is worse than support abstention by `-2.520` and tabular learning by
  `-2.299`; 29 of 30 pairs favor each comparator.
- Tabular final success is `0.751`; support abstention collision rate is
  `0.007`; fuzzy collision rate is `0.244`.

## Adaptive-Gate Compact Validation

- Three environments, five methods, 30 seeds.
- 450 runs, 234,834 raw rows, audit `PASS`.
- Fuzzy versus DQN on FrozenLake 4x4: `+0.291`,
  `[0.244, 0.338]`, t-Holm `p=1.12e-11`.
- CliffWalking has a positive mean and bootstrap interval but a nonsignificant
  mean-based Holm test; Wilcoxon-Holm is `p=1.45e-4`.
- Fuzzy does not significantly beat count gating in any validation task.
- Under complete FourRooms support shift, fuzzy alpha is zero and all 30
  paired seeds favor tabular and support-abstention methods.

## Cost and Support Analysis

- Two environments, five methods, ten seeds.
- 100 runs, 37,081 raw rows, audit `PASS`.
- Application mean decision time ranges from `134 us` for tabular learning to
  `713 us` for fuzzy arbitration.
- Fuzzy exact-memory lower-bound estimates are about `5.38 KiB` in the
  application case and `32.84 KiB` in FourRooms.
- These timings are descriptive and machine-dependent; neural parameter
  storage is excluded from the exact-memory metric.

## Claim Boundary

Count gating can protect against the tested DQN when exact states recur. It is
not a generalization mechanism, does not generally beat tabular Q-learning,
and can fail systematically under support shift. Support abstention repairs
that specific failure without becoming a universal method. Fuzzy adaptive
arbitration improves selected DQN comparisons but neither removes the
zero-support boundary nor generally outperforms simpler memory baselines.
